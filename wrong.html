Common Deepfake Scenarios

Financial Scams

Legitimate lecturers or executives will never ask for credit card information, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers in a video. If a video of a CEO, government official, or educator makes such requests, it's almost certainly a deepfake.

Fake Endorsements

Celebrities or influencers appearing to endorse products they've never mentioned elsewhere. Always verify through official channels.

Manipulated Political Statements

Politicians shown saying things that contradict their known positions or making outrageous claims. Check multiple reputable news sources.

Verification Steps

When you encounter a suspicious video, follow these steps:

  • Pause and assess: Don't react immediately to emotional content
  • Check the source: Is this from a verified account or official channel?
  • Look for duplicates: Search for the same video on other platforms
  • Verify with official statements: Check the person's or organization's official website
  • Use fact-checking tools: Websites like Snopes, Reuters Fact Check, or dedicated deepfake detection tools
  • Consult experts: When in doubt, seek verification from trusted sources

Why This Matters

Deepfake technology is becoming increasingly sophisticated and accessible. Being able to identify manipulated media is crucial for:

  • Protecting yourself from financial scams
  • Maintaining trust in digital information
  • Preserving the integrity of public discourse
  • Preventing the spread of misinformation
