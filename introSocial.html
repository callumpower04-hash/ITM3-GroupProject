Understanding Social Engineering

Learn the fundamentals of how attackers manipulate human psychology to breach security

What is Social Engineering?

By definition the act of deliberately making somebody believe something that is not true so that they provide personal information that may be used to cheat them.

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities

Historical Context

Social engineering tactics have been used throughout history, from the Trojan Horse to modern digital scams. Understanding this history helps us recognize patterns in contemporary attacks.

Deception has been used as a strategy throughout human history

Why Humans Are Vulnerable

Human psychology makes us susceptible to social engineering attacks. Attackers exploit cognitive biases, emotional triggers, and social norms to manipulate their targets.

Cognitive biases and emotional responses make us vulnerable to manipulation

The Impact of Social Engineering

Social engineering attacks can lead to significant damage for individuals and organizations. As malicious actors have a target in mind when they conduct these attacks.

The consequences of successful social engineering attacks can be devastating

Why its important to protect yourself from these attacks

It is important to protect yourself from these attacks as to ensure that no personal information is lost, business information, or financial information. An malicious actor gaining access is a great business risk

Key Concepts and Terminology

Understanding the language of social engineering is essential for recognizing and discussing these threats effectively.

Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.

Pretexting

Creating a fabricated scenario to engage a targeted victim.

Baiting

Offering something enticing to lure victims into a trap.

Understanding key terms helps in recognizing and preventing social engineering attacks
