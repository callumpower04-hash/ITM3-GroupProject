Deepfake Detection Training

Learn to identify AI-generated videos and protect yourself from digital manipulation

üéØ Training Objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

  • Identify common visual indicators of deepfake videos
  • Recognize audio inconsistencies in manipulated media
  • Evaluate contextual clues that suggest digital manipulation
  • Apply critical thinking when encountering suspicious content
  • Protect yourself from deepfake-based scams and misinformation

üëÅÔ∏è Visual Analysis Techniques

Deepfakes often contain subtle visual artifacts that can reveal their artificial nature. Look for these indicators:

üòê Facial Inconsistencies

  • Unnatural or limited blinking
  • Eyes that don't properly focus
  • Mismatched skin tones
  • Strange shadows on the face
  • Blurring around edges of the face

üëÑ Lip Sync Issues

  • Mouth movements don't match audio
  • Unnatural mouth shapes
  • Teeth that look artificial
  • Lack of tongue movement

üí° Lighting Problems

  • Inconsistent lighting on face vs. background
  • Shadows that don't match light sources
  • Reflections in eyes that seem off
  • Unnatural highlights on skin

üîä Audio Analysis

Audio manipulation is often harder to perfect than video. Listen for these clues:

üó£Ô∏è Voice Quality

  • Robotic or synthetic sounding voice
  • Inconsistent tone or pitch
  • Unnatural pauses or pacing
  • Lack of emotional inflection

üéöÔ∏è Audio Artifacts

  • Background noise that doesn't match setting
  • Sudden changes in audio quality
  • Lip movements not matching words
  • Echo or reverb that seems artificial

üß† Contextual Evaluation

Sometimes the content itself reveals manipulation. Consider these factors:

üö© Red Flags

  • Requests for money or sensitive information
  • Content that seems out of character
  • Claims that contradict known facts
  • Only available on suspicious platforms

üîé Verification Steps

  • Check official sources for confirmation
  • Look for the same content on reputable sites
  • Reverse image/video search
  • Use fact-checking websites

Important Note

Legitimate educators, executives, and public figures will never ask for credit card information, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers in videos. If you encounter such requests, it's almost certainly a deepfake scam.
